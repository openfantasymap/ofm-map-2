# Design Context

OpenFantasyMap (`ofm-map-2`) is the primary map-viewer frontend of the Open Fantasy Maps platform: an Angular 20 app that serves deep-time, zoomable maps of fantasy universes (Forgotten Realms/Toril, Golarion, Barovia, starbase decks, etc.) with a scrubbable timeline and per-world layer controls.

## Users

Tabletop RPG **gamemasters and players** exploring large fantasy universes from a desk — for session prep, mid-session reference, world-building, and sharing moments with their table. They come for the worlds, not for software. Secondary audience: Digital Humanities and Public History curious (OHM lineage). Target platform: **desktop first**.

The job-to-be-done is *archival exploration*: "let me look at this world as it was in year 866" or "show my party where the mountain pass is before we run tomorrow's session." It is not a productivity tool; it is a reference artifact.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **atlas, archival, unhurried.**

The interface should feel like a museum's wall-mounted cartographic exhibit, or the typographic plates in a 1930s National Geographic issue — scholarly, quietly serious, confident in its material. Never video-gamey, never costume-fantasy, never dashboard-corporate.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Editorial / National Geographic.** Confident typographic hierarchy. Generous negative space. One rich accent color, used rarely. The UI chrome is a frame around the map — it never competes with the map for attention.

**Theme: warm dark.** `color-scheme: dark` stays, but ink-brown-on-near-black, not trading-terminal black. Think an unlit reading room at dusk, not a HUD.

**Shell treatment: neutral across all worlds.** Chrome is one consistent warm dark frame; each world's color and mood come through the map tiles themselves, not the shell.

### Anti-references (actively rejected)

- Generic SaaS dashboard (sidebar + cards + blue accents + Inter)
- Google Maps clone (floating white cards, blue pins, Roboto)
- D&D Beyond / Roll20 aesthetic (mainstream VTT visual language)
- AI-slop fantasy (purple-to-blue gradients, neon cyan on black, glowing runes)

### Typography

Per the skill's font-selection procedure, all reflex fonts (Fraunces, Crimson, Playfair, Newsreader, Instrument Serif, DM Serif, IBM Plex, Inter, DM Sans, Instrument Sans, etc.) are rejected.

- **Display / section headings**: **Vollkorn** (Google Fonts) — a warm book serif, rigorous but not precious. Carries the "fully-grown cartographic archive" voice.
- **Body / UI**: **Public Sans** (Google Fonts) — intentionally quiet civic sans. Doesn't fight the display, doesn't trend.
- **Numerals / coordinates / dates** (where the atlas-instrument voice wants emphasis): **tabular numerals** from the body sans, letter-spaced slightly. No separate monospace face — monospace as "technical vibes" is explicitly banned.
- Type scale: fixed `rem` in the product UI (not `clamp`), ≥1.25 ratio between steps, 5 steps total.

No pure `#000` / `#fff`. No gradients on text. No `border-left: Npx` accent stripes on anything. No glassmorphism, no drop-shadow rounded rectangles.

### Spacing & Layout

- 4pt scale with semantic tokens: `--space-2xs` 4, `--space-xs` 8, `--space-sm` 12, `--space-md` 16, `--space-lg` 24, `--space-xl` 32, `--space-2xl` 48, `--space-3xl` 64, `--space-4xl` 96.
- `gap` over margins for sibling spacing.
- Cap body text at `65–75ch`. The `info` description in the landing sidebar is the main offender today.
- Break the grid intentionally in the world-picker — varied tile sizes already hint at this; we lean in.

### Motion

Used for state changes only, never decoration. `ease-out-quart` default, 150–220ms. Only `transform` and `opacity` animate. Reduced-motion respected even though accessibility was deprioritized — it's free.

No bounce, no elastic.

## Accessibility posture

User explicitly deprioritized AA/AAA targets. I will *not* break focus indicators (doing so harms everyone including non-disabled keyboard users), and I will keep `prefers-reduced-motion` honored, but I will not chase contrast ratios at the expense of the warm-dark atmosphere.

## Design Principles (guiding rules)

1. **The map is the subject.** Chrome is a frame. Chrome never competes for attention.
2. **Warm, not cool.** Dark, sepia-leaning. Ember, not neon.
3. **One accent, used rarely.** `--ofm-accent` appears at interaction moments. Not as decoration.
4. **Typography does the hierarchy.** Size, weight, and case — not color, gradient, or glow.
5. **Negative space is a feature.** Let the interface breathe. Density is not a virtue here.
